Representing Proteins

Predicting and discovering protein structures is vital to our understanding of a major portion of the vast expanse of cellular functions. Jane Richardson invented the ribbon diagram, an intuitive way for us to draw protein structures and conceptualize their function.

Currently, the RCSB protein data bank uses a file format .mmCIF to represent the positions of atoms in molecules, with which we can construct the spatial structure of proteins. Being able to characterize proteins in a standard format is incredibly useful and paves the way for understanding biochemical pathways more accurately through computation.
